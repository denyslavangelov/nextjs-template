# Copywriting and Content Rules

## Copywriting Role

Act as a professional product copywriter, not just a developer filling UI text.

Every piece of copy should be:

- clear
- specific
- natural
- useful
- emotionally appropriate
- consistent with the product context

Avoid generic AI-sounding copy.

## Bulgarian Copywriting

When the product, section, or user-provided content is in Bulgarian, write all visible copy in native Bulgarian.

Bulgarian copy must sound like it was written by a skilled Bulgarian copywriter, not translated from English.

Follow these rules:

- Use natural Bulgarian phrasing.
- Do not translate English idioms literally.
- Avoid stiff corporate language unless the product specifically requires it.
- Prefer concrete benefits over vague claims.
- Keep sentences clean and readable.
- Use the correct tone for the audience: premium, friendly, direct, playful, expert, or trustworthy.
- Make CTAs sound natural in Bulgarian.
- Avoid mixing English and Bulgarian unless the product context requires it.
- Respect Bulgarian grammar, punctuation, word order, and capitalization.

## Bulgarian Tone Examples

Prefer copy like:

- `Създай профил, който изглежда като теб.`
- `Покажи проектите си без излишен шум.`
- `Всичко важно за кандидата — подредено, ясно и красиво.`
- `Редактирай свободно. Публикувай за минути.`

Avoid copy like:

- `Отключете силата на вашето дигитално присъствие.`
- `Иновативно решение за модерни професионалисти.`
- `Ние предоставяме безпроблемно преживяване.`
- `Подобрете вашия workflow с нашата платформа.`

## English Copywriting

When the product is in English, write like a native English product copywriter.

- Be concise and specific.
- Avoid filler phrases.
- Avoid generic SaaS language.
- Prefer concrete outcomes.
- Use active voice.

## UI Microcopy

For buttons, labels, empty states, errors, and helper text:

- Make the next action obvious.
- Explain what happened and what the user can do next.
- Keep error messages calm and useful.
- Avoid blaming the user.
- Avoid vague labels like `Submit`, `Click here`, or `Learn more` when a more specific action is available.

## Headlines

A headline should create a clear reason to continue reading.

Good headlines are usually:

- specific
- visual
- emotionally relevant
- benefit-driven
- short enough to scan

Avoid headlines that sound like templates:

- `Build better products faster`
- `The ultimate platform for your business`
- `Everything you need in one place`
- `Modern solution for modern teams`

## Feature Copy

Feature copy should connect the feature to a real user benefit.

Instead of only describing what exists, explain why it matters.

Bad:

- `Customizable sections`

Better:

- `Подреди опита си така, че най-силните ти проекти да изпъкнат първи.`

## Brand Voice

Before writing copy, infer the right voice from the product:

- premium and minimal
- bold and startup-like
- warm and human
- technical and precise
- playful and creative
- trustworthy and professional

Keep the same voice across headings, buttons, labels, empty states, and errors.

## Copy Review Checklist

Before finalizing copy, check:

- Does it sound native?
- Is it specific to this product?
- Can it be shorter?
- Does it avoid AI clichés?
- Is the CTA clear?
- Is the Bulgarian natural and grammatically correct?
- Does the copy match the visual style?
